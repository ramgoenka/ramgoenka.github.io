About me

Hi! My name is Ram Goenka and I am a (incoming) first year masters student at the University of Pittsburgh in the Department of Statistics.

Prior to joining Pitt, I was an undergraduate student in Mathematics and Statistics at the University of Illinois (UIUC). I worked as a research assistant at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications on computational problems relating to the mathematical and statistical modeling of vector-borne diseases, specifically the West Nile Virus (WNV), mentored by Professor Rebecca Lee Smith.

My research interests lie at the intersection of statistics, probability and theoretical ECE. I am primarily interested in statistical learning and random processes as well as their intersections with inference problems in signal processing, detection and communication theory.
